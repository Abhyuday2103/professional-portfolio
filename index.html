Artifact · Workshop One

Artifact 01

Professional self-assessment

Introduction

Before you can lead anyone else through a change, you have to run an honest audit on yourself. This artifact is that audit, and I am leading my portfolio with it on purpose. A portfolio that only shows wins is a brochure. This one starts with the gaps.

Description

In Workshop One I completed two diagnostic self-assessments, one measuring readiness to integrate AI and machine learning into an organization, the other measuring change management capability. I then analyzed the results against the course readings and wrote a candid account of where I am strong, where I am not, and where the instruments themselves fall short.

Objective

To set a baseline I can return to later, to name my real growth areas instead of the comfortable ones, and to show that I can assess my own capability with the same rigor I bring to a sales territory or a key account.

Process

The two forms revealed different things because I scored so differently on each. On the AI/ML integration assessment my strengths were promoting the value of AI and ML, crafting a vision for adoption, and keeping up with the field, which tracks with years spent selling stone to skeptical fabricators, a job that is essentially evangelism. My growth areas were ethical awareness, preparing people for AI-driven change, and understanding data requirements. The ethics one stung because it is true. I have built tools at work, including a pricing calculator the sales team uses, and I only ever asked whether they worked, never what could go wrong.

On the change management assessment my strengths were stakeholder engagement, performance evaluation, and problem-solving, all sharpened by managing a small team and learning to read where people actually are versus where they say they are. My growth areas were formal frameworks, risk management, and facing resistance head-on. I have led change by instinct but could not have named a single framework before this course, and my instinct when someone resists is to route around them rather than through them. The Center for Creative Leadership (2024) names exactly this, that unsuccessful change leaders avoid difficult stakeholders instead of influencing them, and I recognized myself in the line.

My read on the instruments is mixed. Their value, as Change Strategists (2023) notes, is creating a benchmark you can return to later. But both measure self-perception, not behavior, so I could rate myself a 5 on everything and no one would know the difference. I caught my own inconsistency, rating ethical awareness a 1 while scoring 16 out of 20 on the dedicated ethics section, which tells me the overlapping items are not measuring cleanly.

If I revised them I would require a one-sentence example behind every rating, since Change Strategists (2023) ties real assessment to behavioral evidence and past performance, and a bare number invites wishful thinking. I would also add the most important finding from the Center for Creative Leadership (2024): whether you communicate the why behind a change, not just the what. In my experience connecting a change to what people already care about is the entire game, and neither form asks about it.

Tools and technologies used

AI/ML Integration Readiness self-assessment, the Change Management capability self-assessment, and Claude Fable 5 (Anthropic), which I used to help organize the analysis. The scores, workplace examples, and conclusions are my own, and I reviewed and edited the final content to make sure it represents my views.

Value proposition

Unique value. Most people cannot name their own blind spots. This artifact shows that I can, and that I use frameworks and evidence to do it rather than gut feel. That is the same discipline that turns a self-assessment into a development plan and a sales gap into a closed deal.
Relevance. For any employer or partner betting on me to lead change, whether that means adopting AI tools or expanding a business into a new market, the first question is whether I know what I do not know. This is the proof.
